# Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 22, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Journal.IO ("Journal.IO," "we," "our," or "us") collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Journal.IO mobile app, website, and related services (collectively, the "Services").

Journal.IO is a behavioral journaling product designed to help users reflect, notice patterns, and build practical habits over time. It is not a medical service, and its AI-supported outputs are intended to be supportive, non-clinical, and uncertainty-aware.

By using the Services, you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this Privacy Policy.

## 1. Information We Collect

We collect information you provide directly, information created through your use of the Services, and limited technical information needed to operate and secure the Services.

### A. Account and profile information

Depending on how you sign up and use Journal.IO, we may collect:

- your name
- your email address
- your phone number, if provided
- authentication method or linked provider information, such as email or Google sign-in
- profile setup information, including journaling goals, onboarding answers, reminder preferences, and privacy or AI preferences
- profile image or avatar settings
- subscription or premium status

### B. Journal and wellness-related information

When you use the Services, we may collect and store:

- journal entries, titles, and entry content
- entry metadata such as timestamps, favorites, tags, selected prompts, and attached image references
- mood check-ins
- reminders and reminder settings
- streak records, usage stats, and derived insights
- structured AI-generated or rules-based outputs created from your journal activity, such as summaries, tags, trend signals, or pattern-related insights

### C. Purchase and subscription information

If you purchase a premium subscription or restore a purchase, we and our subscription infrastructure providers may process limited subscription data such as:

- plan type
- entitlement or subscription status
- purchase source or store
- purchase timing and restore events

We do not store full payment card numbers in the Journal.IO application database.

### D. Technical and operational information

We may collect technical information needed to run, secure, and improve the Services, such as:

- device or app version information
- log data related to authentication, security events, and service reliability
- basic usage events related to app flows, locked premium surfaces, reminders, and subscription actions

We do not intentionally log raw journal text in ordinary production operations.

## 2. How We Use Information

We use personal information to:

- create and manage your account
- authenticate you and maintain your session
- provide journaling, mood tracking, reminders, insights, streaks, and related product features
- personalize your experience based on your settings, onboarding answers, and recent activity
- generate structured tags, prompts, reflections, or insight summaries when AI-supported features are enabled and available for your account
- process premium access, restore purchases, and keep subscription state in sync
- provide customer support and respond to requests
- export your data or delete your account when requested
- detect, prevent, and investigate security issues, fraud, abuse, and misuse
- comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms
- improve product quality, reliability, and feature performance

## 3. AI Processing

Journal.IO may use third-party AI service providers, including OpenAI, to process limited portions of your content when AI-supported features are enabled and available for your account.

This may include processing:

- journal entry text
- excerpts from recent entries
- structured context derived from your writing or product activity

We use this processing to support features such as:

- prompt generation
- tag suggestion
- quick reflections
- weekly or trend-based insight summaries

AI-supported features are designed to be supportive and behavior-focused. They are not intended to diagnose any condition, provide medical advice, or replace professional care.

If AI-supported features are disabled for your account where that control is available, we will stop using new content for those AI features on a going-forward basis. Journal.IO may also clear cached AI summaries associated with that preference change.

We do not sell your personal information or journal content.

We may share information in the following situations:

### A. Service providers

We may share information with vendors and service providers that help us operate the Services, such as providers for:

- cloud hosting and infrastructure

- authentication
- email delivery
- subscription management and purchase verification
- AI processing
- analytics, logging, and operational monitoring

These providers are authorized to process information on our behalf only as needed to provide services to us, subject to their own terms and applicable contractual or legal obligations.

### B. Legal, safety, and compliance reasons

We may disclose information if we believe it is reasonably necessary to:

- comply with applicable law, regulation, legal process, or enforceable government request
- protect the rights, property, or safety of Journal.IO, our users, or others
- investigate fraud, abuse, security incidents, or violations of our terms

### C. Business transfers

If Journal.IO is involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, sale of assets, or similar transaction, information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to applicable law.

## 5. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the Services, maintain your account, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, and protect the security and integrity of the Services.

In general:

- account and journal-related data is retained while your account remains active
- if you request account deletion, we will delete or de-identify active production records associated with your account, except where limited retention is required for legal, security, fraud-prevention, backup, or operational reasons
- residual copies may remain in backups or logs for a limited period before routine deletion

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect personal information.

These measures include:

- encryption in transit using HTTPS and TLS
- encrypted or encryption-capable storage controls at rest

- restricted access to production systems
- logging and monitoring for important security and operational failures

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
